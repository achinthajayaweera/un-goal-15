UN Goal 15 – Life on Land

UN Sustainable Development Goal 15 – Life on Land is a global mission to protect, restore, and promote the sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems. This includes responsibly managing forests, fighting desertification, reversing land degradation, and halting the alarming loss of biodiversity. Land ecosystems—such as forests, grasslands, wetlands, and mountains—are essential to human life, providing food, clean water, air, and shelter. However, overexploitation, deforestation, pollution, and climate change have put these systems under serious threat, with devastating consequences for both nature and society.


Forests alone are home to more than 80% of terrestrial species and support the livelihoods of over a billion people worldwide. Yet, each year, millions of hectares are lost due to illegal logging, unsustainable agriculture, and urban expansion. The degradation of land and biodiversity contributes to climate change and increases the risk of natural disasters like floods and droughts. UN Goal 15 calls for global action to manage these vital ecosystems more responsibly—through reforestation, land rehabilitation, and stronger conservation policies—to ensure long-term balance between nature and human needs.


Beyond environmental protection, Goal 15 has a direct impact on achieving many other global goals, such as ending hunger, improving health, and reducing poverty. Healthy ecosystems support sustainable agriculture, clean water supplies, and resilient communities. By educating communities, promoting inclusive land management practices, and enforcing environmental laws, the goal aims to build a future where people and planet thrive together. Achieving Goal 15 is not just about preserving nature—it's about securing a healthy, livable planet for generations to come.
